Republic of the Philippines

This DPA is entered in connection with the Venue Partner Agreement AKD-VPA-005 and governs the processing of personal data of end users accessing the Platform at the Venue Partner's Deployment Site.

---

## ARTICLE 1 – SCOPE AND PURPOSE

1. This DPA governs the processing of personal data of end users who access the Platform at the Venue Partner's Deployment Site.
2. AKD is the Personal Information Controller (PIC) responsible for determining the purposes and means of processing personal data collected through the Platform.
3. The Venue Partner is a Personal Information Processor (PIP) to the limited extent that it provides the physical premises and internet connectivity enabling Platform operation.

## ARTICLE 2 – DATA PROCESSED

| Data Category | Description | Controller |
|---------------|-------------|------------|
| Device Identifiers | MAC addresses, device type, OS version | AKD (PIC) |
| Network Data | IP addresses, session timestamps, connection duration | AKD (PIC) |
| Ad Interaction Data | Ad views, completion status, ad format type | AKD (PIC) |
| Authentication Data | Session tokens (no passwords stored) | AKD (PIC) |
| Optional Registration Data | Mobile number, name (if voluntarily provided) | AKD (PIC) |

The Venue Partner does not independently collect, store, or process any personal data listed above. All personal data is collected and processed exclusively by AKD's Platform systems.

## ARTICLE 3 – PIP OBLIGATIONS OF VENUE PARTNER

1. Process personal data only in accordance with AKD's documented instructions.
2. Not process personal data for any purpose other than enabling the Platform to operate at the Deployment Site.
3. Not transfer, share, sell, or disclose end-user personal data to any third party without AKD's prior written authorization.
4. Implement reasonable organizational measures to ensure persons with physical access to the Deployment Site comply with data protection obligations.
5. Notify AKD within twenty-four (24) hours of: unauthorized Equipment access; suspected personal data breach; or government/law enforcement request for personal data.
6. Upon termination, certify in writing that it has not retained any personal data obtained through the Platform.

## ARTICLE 4 – PIC OBLIGATIONS OF AKD

1. AKD shall collect and process personal data only for the purposes described in Schedule 1 and per RA 10173 and its IRR.
2. AKD shall implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures including encryption, access controls, and regular security assessments.
3. AKD shall provide end users with a clear Privacy Policy disclosing data types, purposes, retention periods, and user rights.
4. AKD shall register with the NPC and designate a Data Protection Officer (DPO) as required.
5. AKD shall conduct a Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) in connection with the Platform's data processing activities.
6. AKD shall respect and facilitate the exercise of end-user rights under RA 10173.

## ARTICLE 5 – DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

| Data Type | Retention Period | Reason |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Connection Logs (device ID, IP, timestamps) | 180 days (up to 12 months if required by law) | Security, fraud prevention, legal compliance |
| Ad Session Data (views, completion) | 24 months | Revenue reporting, audit, advertiser billing |
| Optional Registration Data | Duration of account + 12 months | Service delivery |
| Breach and Security Incident Records | 5 years | Legal compliance, NPC reporting |

## ARTICLE 6 – DATA BREACH RESPONSE

1. AKD shall: (a) conduct internal investigation within 24 hours; (b) notify NPC within 72 hours for notifiable breaches per RA 10173 and NPC Circular 16-03; (c) notify affected data subjects as required; (d) submit full breach report to NPC within 30 days.
2. The Venue Partner shall cooperate fully with AKD's breach investigation.

## SCHEDULE 1 – PROCESSING ACTIVITIES

| Processing Activity | Purpose | Legal Basis |
|---------------------|---------|-------------|
| Collection of device identifiers and connection logs | Network access control, security, fraud prevention, legal compliance | Legitimate interest; legal obligation (RA 10175) |

| Optional collection of mobile number or email | User account, notifications | Consent |
