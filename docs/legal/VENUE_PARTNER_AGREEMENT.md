## ARTICLE 1 – DEFINITIONS

1. **"Platform"** – AKD's proprietary WiFi advertising portal system, including all software, firmware, dashboards, and related services, functioning as a network access management and advertising delivery system.
2. **"Equipment"** – Hardware per Annex A: mini-PC controller, WiFi AP, UPS, PoE injector, cabling, and accessories.
3. **"Deployment Site"** – The Partner's specific premises per Annex A.
4. **"Ad Session"** – A single complete advertisement viewing by one end user resulting in internet access.
5. **"Net Verified Views"** – Ad Sessions validated by AKD's server-side anti-fraud system, excluding bot traffic, duplicate sessions within 60 minutes per MAC address, and invalid sessions.
6. **"Connection Logs"** – Records of device identifiers, MAC addresses, IP addresses, session timestamps, and related metadata.
7. **"Monthly Fee"** – Fixed monthly charge per Article 6.
8. **"Revenue Share"** – Per-Net-Verified-View payment from AKD to the Partner per Article 6, payable only from advertising revenue actually received by AKD.
9. **"Business Day"** – Any weekday (Monday–Saturday) that is not a Philippine public holiday.
10. **"PIC"** – Personal Information Controller under RA 10173.
11. **"PIP"** – Personal Information Processor under RA 10173.
12. **"Advertising Inventory"** – The pool of active advertiser campaigns available for Platform delivery at any given time.

## ARTICLE 2 – PLATFORM CLASSIFICATION AND NTC DISCLAIMER

The Parties acknowledge and agree that: (a) The Platform functions solely as a network access management and advertising delivery system operating on top of the Partner's independently obtained internet service; (b) AKD does not own or operate telecommunications infrastructure and does not act as an ISP or public telecommunications entity; (c) The Partner is the internet subscriber of record; (d) End users are not charged any monetary consideration for internet connectivity. Advertising viewing is a condition of access to the Platform's promotional WiFi service and does not constitute the sale, resale, or provision of telecommunications services, value-added telecom services, or paid internet connectivity; (e) If any Philippine regulatory authority issues a binding determination requiring specific licensing for the Platform, AKD shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain such authorization or modify the Platform accordingly, and may suspend service pending resolution without liability.

## ARTICLE 6 – FINANCIAL TERMS

### 6.3 Revenue Share Payment Conditions

1. **Actual Receipt Condition.** Revenue Share is payable only from advertising revenue actually received by AKD from Advertisers. AKD is not obligated to pay Revenue Share for amounts that remain unpaid, disputed, refunded, or subject to advertiser chargeback at the time of Partner payment.
2. **Good Faith Collection.** AKD shall use commercially reasonable efforts to collect advertising revenue from Advertisers and shall not intentionally delay or withhold advertiser payments in order to avoid Revenue Share obligations to the Partner.
3. **Minimum Views Threshold.** Revenue Share is payable only in months where the Deployment Site generates a minimum of 2,000 Net Verified Views, computed per Deployment Site per calendar month across all ad formats. The minimum threshold shall NOT apply in months where AKD fails to deliver sufficient Advertising Inventory despite adequate user traffic at the Deployment Site.
4. **Inventory Allocation.** Advertising Inventory is not guaranteed. AKD may allocate campaigns among Deployment Sites based on targeting requirements, campaign availability, and operational factors.
5. If AKD's Advertising Inventory is insufficient to reach 2,000 views through no fault of the Partner, AKD shall notify the Partner; such month shall not count against the Partner's performance record.
6. Revenue subject to fraud investigation, payment processor reversal, advertiser chargeback, or regulatory refund order shall not be considered revenue received by AKD for Revenue Share purposes. AKD shall notify the Partner of any such deduction in the relevant monthly statement.
7. AKD shall not be liable for advertiser insolvency, bankruptcy, or non-payment beyond commercially reasonable collection efforts.

### 6.4 Reporting and Disputes

1. AKD provides monthly statements by the 10th of the following month: Ad Sessions, Net Verified Views, Revenue Share computation, and Monthly Fee status.
2. AKD Server Records are prima facie evidence. Partner may dispute in writing within seven (7) Business Days with specific grounds. Undisputed statements are deemed accepted. AKD responds within ten (10) Business Days.
3. Upon written request, the Partner may receive a summarized extract of timestamped Net Verified Views for a specified month for verification purposes, provided that such extract does not expose confidential advertiser identities, platform security information, or proprietary fraud detection data.

### 6.5 Revenue Share Payment

Undisputed Revenue Share for Month N disbursed by the 15th of Month N+1 via GCash, bank transfer, or mutually agreed method.

### 6.6 Taxes

1. All fees exclude VAT and applicable taxes unless expressly stated otherwise.
2. Each Party is responsible for its own income taxes. Withholding tax shall be deducted and remitted with BIR Form 2307 issued within the prescribed period.
3. AKD issues Official Receipts for all Monthly Fees per BIR requirements.

## ARTICLE 7 – END-USER INTERNET ACTIVITY AND LEGAL COMPLIANCE

1. AKD shall not be liable for unlawful acts by end users, including violations of RA 10175 (Cybercrime Prevention Act), RA 8293 (Intellectual Property Code), RA 9775 (Anti-Child Pornography Act), RA 11930 (Anti-OSAEC Act), or any applicable Philippine law.
2. Neither Party shall knowingly allow or facilitate unlawful internet activity at the Deployment Site.
3. Both Parties shall cooperate with lawful government investigations, law enforcement requests, and court orders. The receiving Party shall notify the other within twenty-four (24) hours where legally permissible.

## ARTICLE 8 – MINOR PROTECTION

1. The Platform may be accessed by users under eighteen (18) years of age. AKD shall implement reasonable technical measures including advertising content restrictions and server-side content filtering.
2. AKD does not collect or infer age data for the purpose of targeted advertising, and does not profile or target advertisements based on inferred user age. All advertisements must comply with AKD's all-age content standards.
3. AKD does not knowingly collect personal data from children under thirteen (13) years of age without verifiable parental consent, per RA 10173 and NPC guidelines.
4. Partner shall not knowingly permit harmful online activity involving minors and shall notify AKD immediately if such activity is discovered.

## ARTICLE 9 – CONNECTION LOGS AND DATA RETENTION

1. AKD may collect and retain Connection Logs for: security monitoring, fraud prevention, Platform optimization, and compliance with RA 10175 and RA 10173.
2. Connection Logs shall be retained for a minimum of one hundred eighty (180) days, extendable to twelve (12) months where required for ongoing investigations, court orders, or law enforcement requests.
3. Some mobile devices may utilize randomized MAC addresses, which may limit the accuracy or continuity of long-term device identification in Connection Logs. AKD makes no representation as to the completeness of device identification where randomized MAC addresses are used.
4. Partner shall not access, copy, modify, or delete Connection Logs stored by AKD's systems.
5. AKD shall respond to lawful government requests for Connection Logs in accordance with applicable law and notify the Partner where legally permissible.

## ARTICLE 10 – DATA PRIVACY, PIC/PIP ROLES, AND SECURITY

1. **PIC/PIP Roles.** AKD is the Personal Information Controller (PIC). The Partner is a Personal Information Processor (PIP) solely to the extent of providing the physical site and internet connectivity. The Partner shall not independently collect, process, share, or use personal data generated through the Platform.
2. AKD implements reasonable technical and organizational security measures per RA 10173 and its IRR.
3. **Data Breach.** Each Party notifies the other within forty-eight (48) hours of discovering a personal data breach. Both Parties cooperate in NPC notification within seventy-two (72) hours for notifiable breaches.
4. Each Party designates a data privacy contact or DPO and provides details upon request. AKD registers with the NPC as required.
5. This Agreement is read together with AKD's Data Processing Agreement (AKD-DPA-001) and Privacy Policy (AKD-POL-004).

## ARTICLE 11 – MINIMUM SITE ACTIVITY

1. Advertising revenue depends on actual end-user traffic, advertiser demand, and market conditions. AKD does not guarantee any minimum advertising revenue, view volume, or Revenue Share.
2. Partner shall not hold AKD liable for Revenue Share shortfalls from: insufficient foot traffic; low advertiser demand; seasonal variations; or factors outside AKD's reasonable control.
3. The Monthly Fee is independent of and not contingent on Revenue Share received.

## ARTICLE 11B – TRAFFIC MANIPULATION AND THRESHOLD INTEGRITY

1. Any Ad Sessions determined by AKD's fraud detection systems to be artificially generated, manipulated, or otherwise invalid shall not count toward the minimum 2,000 Net Verified Views threshold and shall not be included in Revenue Share calculations.
2. If AKD determines that a Deployment Site has been subject to systematic traffic manipulation, AKD may: (a) suspend Revenue Share for the affected month(s); (b) retroactively recalculate Revenue Share excluding manipulated sessions; and (c) terminate this Agreement.
3. The Partner shall not be penalized for Invalid Traffic attributable solely to end-user device behavior (e.g., MAC address randomization or automated OS connectivity checks) that is outside the Partner's control.

## ARTICLE 12 – ADVERTISING CONTINUITY

1. AKD shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain active Advertising Inventory at all Deployment Sites.
2. **Advertising Fallback.** If Advertising Inventory is unavailable at a Deployment Site for sixty (60) consecutive days due to AKD's inability to secure advertiser campaigns (and not due to Partner's breach, regulatory suspension, force majeure, or Partner-caused network issues), AKD shall, at its option: (a) convert the Platform to a standard WiFi access portal without advertisements; or (b) reduce the Monthly Fee by fifty percent (50%) until Advertising Inventory is restored.
3. AKD shall notify the Partner of any advertising fallback activation within seven (7) Business Days of triggering the sixty (60) day threshold.

## ARTICLE 13 – PARTNER OBLIGATIONS AND CONDUCT

1. Partner shall not manipulate or artificially inflate Ad Session data through any means.
2. Partner shall ensure the Deployment Site is accessible during operating hours per Annex A.
3. **Non-compete (Site-Specific).** During the term, Partner shall not permit installation at the Deployment Site of any competing captive-portal WiFi advertising system.
4. Partner shall maintain confidentiality of AKD's pricing, technical systems, and business methods.
5. AKD has sole discretion over advertisement selection, scheduling, and content.

## ARTICLE 14 – SITE ACCESS

1. Partner shall provide AKD and authorized representatives reasonable access for: installation, maintenance, upgrade, inspection, and Equipment removal.
2. AKD shall provide forty-eight (48) hours' advance written notice for scheduled visits; no notice required for emergencies, suspected fraud, or imminent Equipment damage.
3. Unjustified refusal of access constitutes material breach. If not remedied within seven (7) Business Days of written notice, AKD may terminate and recover Equipment.

## ARTICLE 15 – AKD OBLIGATIONS

1. AKD shall maintain the Platform targeting monthly uptime of at least ninety percent (90%), excluding scheduled maintenance, force majeure, upstream internet outages, and Partner-caused disruptions.
2. AKD shall provide technical support during Business Hours (Monday–Saturday, 9:00 AM–6:00 PM PST).
3. AKD shall provide monthly performance statements per Article 6.4.
4. AKD shall ensure advertisement content complies with applicable Philippine advertising standards.
5. AKD shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain active advertiser relationships and Advertising Inventory.

## ARTICLE 16 – REGULATORY COOPERATION

3. Partner shall cooperate with regulatory investigations as lawfully required.

## ARTICLE 17 – REGULATORY CHANGE

1. If Philippine law or regulation enacted or amended after the effective date materially affects Platform legality or operation, AKD may modify the Platform, Agreement, or operations to maintain compliance upon reasonable notice.
2. If compliance becomes commercially impracticable, AKD may suspend or terminate at affected Deployment Sites without liability, subject to refund of prepaid Monthly Fees for the unused period.

## ARTICLE 18 – TERM AND RENEWAL

1. This Agreement commences on the installation date in Annex A and continues for eighteen (18) months.
2. Renewal requires mutual written agreement; fees and Revenue Share rates subject to renegotiation.
3. Continued service after the Initial Term without a signed renewal is month-to-month, terminable by either Party with thirty (30) days' written notice.

## ARTICLE 19 – TERMINATION

### 19.1 Early Termination by Partner

1. Partner may terminate before the Initial Term with thirty (30) days' notice, subject to an early termination fee of fifty percent (50%) of Monthly Fee × remaining months.
2. **Reduced Fee.** If Deployment Site becomes legally unavailable due to: premises lease termination (not caused by Partner); government order; or force majeure exceeding thirty (30) days — termination fee is the lower of three (3) months' Monthly Fees or fifty percent (50%) of remaining Monthly Fees.

### 19.2 Termination by AKD for Cause

AKD may terminate immediately for: non-payment for two (2) consecutive months after grace; unresolved material breach after fourteen (14) days' notice; Ad Session fraud; illegal Platform use; Equipment loss or unauthorized disposal; installation of competing systems; or unjustified refusal of access.

### 19.3 Termination Without Cause (Post-Initial Term)

Either Party may terminate without cause with thirty (30) days' written notice after the Initial Term.

### 19.4 AKD Without Cause (During Initial Term)

AKD refunds prepaid Monthly Fees for the unused month portion; no penalty to Partner.

### 19.5 Partner for AKD Default

If AKD materially defaults and fails to cure within fifteen (15) Business Days of written notice, Partner may terminate without early termination fee.

### 19.6 Equipment Return

Partner returns all Equipment within seven (7) calendar days. AKD's site access right per Article 14 applies.

## ARTICLE 20 – INSURANCE AND RISK

1. AKD insures Equipment against manufacturing defects and transit damage during delivery.
2. Partner bears risk of loss, theft, fire, flood, and physical damage while Equipment is in Partner custody.
3. Partner is encouraged to obtain property insurance covering electronic equipment at the Deployment Site.

## ARTICLE 21 – LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

1. AKD's total aggregate liability to the Partner shall not exceed the greater of: (a) total Monthly Fees paid in the three (3) months preceding the event; or (b) the current book value of the Equipment at the Deployment Site at the time of the event.

3. Nothing limits liability for fraud, willful misconduct, or death/personal injury caused by AKD's negligence.
